Working with CN61 and CN64 statements

To work with CN61 and CN64 statements, click the CN61/CN64 tab on the Home page. Using this function, you can:

  • Create new CN61 forms (2014 or 2017 format)
  • Create new CN64 forms
  • View and execute actions on existing forms

Roles and workflow

A postal organization can assume either of the following roles during the workflow, depending on the action they are performing:

  • Preparing operator (PO)
  • Corresponding operator (CO)

The preparing operator creates new forms, and the corresponding operator disputes or validates them.

The different stages of the workflow can be summarized as follows:

  1. Statement is created (status: Drafted) and submitted by preparing operator (status: Submitted).
  2. The operators can accept the statement (status: Accepted) or discuss it (status: Under Discussion CO / PO).
  3. The statement can be accepted by one or both parties (status: Under Discussion Accepted by CO / PO), or disputed by one or both parties (status: In Dispute CO / PO). At this point it can be accepted (status: In Dispute Accepted By CO / PO) or sent to the UPU for arbitration if disputed (status: In Arbitration).

The statuses are set in QSF according to the actions executed by the preparing and corresponding operators when working with the statements in the procedures below.

Procedures

Creating a new CN61

  1. Click Create CN61 v2017 or Create CN61 v2014, depending on the version required.
  2. In the Mail flow tab:
    1. Complete the details of the CN and the preparing and corresponding operators. All fields with a red asterisk are mandatory.

    2. Enter the amounts for the different types of mail received and mail dispatched.

  3. When you have finished completing the fields, click Save to save the form. You can then click the Terminal dues tab to see the terminal dues calculated, based on the information entered in the form and the bilateral agreement. Enter any corrections to the relevant fields and click Save.

  4. If you are creating a CN61 v2014, click the Comment tab to attach any additional CN documents.

  5. If you are ready to submit the form, select Submit CN from the CN Action field and click Execute action.

Creating a new CN64

  1. Click Create CN64.
  2. In the Terminal dues tab:
    1. Complete the details of the CN and preparing and corresponding operators. All fields with a red asterisk are mandatory.

    2. Enter the terminal dues for the preparing and corresponding operators.

  3. Click the CDI tab to enter CDI details.
  4. When you have finished completing the fields, click Save to save the form.
  5. If you are ready to submit the form, select Submit CN from the CN Action field and click Execute action.

Viewing or updating forms prepared or received by your organization

  1. Click Manage CN61 / CN64. Forms your organization has prepared or received are listed. You can filter the list by clicking the drop-down arrow at the top.
  2. If you want to edit a form, click the Edit icon next to the form you want to update.
  3. Update the details as in the relevant section above and execute a new action if required. The actions you execute depend on what stage of the workflow the form has reached. For example, if the status of the form is:
    • Drafted, you can execute action Submit CN
    • Submitted, you can execute action Accept CN or Discuss CN
    • Under Discussion CO / PO, you can execute action Accept CN, Discuss CN or Dispute CN

    CNs which have been accepted by both parties (i.e. are in Accepted state) have reached the end of the workflow and can no longer have actions executed on them.
