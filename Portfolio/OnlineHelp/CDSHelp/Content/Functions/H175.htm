Validate anonymous declaration for unidentified packets

Mail items that do not have the S10 identifier, also referred to as "blind packets", are not subject to EMSEVT (track and trace) but they might be carrying goods that require a declaration form and an ITMATT message. Customers either take the blind packets to the post office with a printed anonymous declaration form that they entered online using the Kiosk, or, drop the blind packets directly in the post box after sticking the declaration form to the item.

You use the Validate anonymous declaration for unidentified packets function to reconcile the anonymous declaration data with CDS and send the ITMATT message for blind packets.

All anonymous declarations that have not been reconciled with CDS within 30 days of the creation date are automatically purged from the database.

To validate an anonymous declaration for unidentified packets

  1. Enter the declaration ID.
  2. Click Store. The system marks the item as "being in the postal supply chain" and triggers generation of the ITMATT message.
